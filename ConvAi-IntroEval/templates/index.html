Speaking Tips & Guidelines

Speech Delivery

  • Speak clearly and at a moderate pace - Ensure your words are easily understood
  • Use proper grammar and complete sentences - Avoid filler words and incomplete thoughts
  • Maintain a confident and professional tone - Sound enthusiastic but not overly casual
  • Structure your introduction logically - Follow a clear past-present-future progression

Essential Information to Include

  • Your name and current status - Introduce yourself clearly
  • Educational background - Mention your degree, institution, and graduation year
  • Technical skills and expertise - Highlight your key competencies
  • Work experience or projects - Share relevant practical experience
  • Career goals and aspirations - Explain what you're looking for
  • Unique qualities or interests - What makes you stand out

Connect to Your Target Role

  • Align your skills with job requirements - Show how your abilities match the role
  • Mention relevant technologies or tools - Demonstrate technical fit
  • Highlight problem-solving abilities - Share examples of challenges you've solved
  • Express genuine interest in the field - Show passion for your chosen career path

Example Structure

Opening: "Hello, I'm [Name], a [current status] graduating from [Institution]..."

Background: "I've completed my [degree] with specialization in [field]..."

Skills & Experience: "I'm proficient in [technologies] and have worked on [projects/internships]..."

Goals: "I'm seeking opportunities in [field] where I can apply my skills in [specific areas]..."

Closing: "I'm excited about [specific aspect] and ready to contribute to [type of organization]."

Remember

  • Keep it concise but comprehensive (2-3 minutes)
  • Practice beforehand to sound natural
  • Be authentic and genuine
  • Focus on achievements and future potential

Evaluation Rubrics - How You'll Be Graded

Introduction Rating Overview

Your spoken introduction will be evaluated on 4 key criteria for a total of 10 points:

Grammar & Clarity

3.0 points

How clearly and grammatically you communicate

3.0 Fluent, grammatically appropriate, clear and coherent sentences throughout
2.5 Mostly fluent and clear, minor grammatical errors that don't hinder understanding
2.0 Understandable, but with noticeable grammatical errors or clarity issues
1.0-1.5 Several errors in grammar or unclear sentences, making it difficult to follow

Structure

1.5 points

How well-organized and logical your introduction flows

1.5 Effective organization that creates coherent impression, regardless of specific format
1.0 Generally logical flow, but might have minor jumps or could be more organized
0.5 Some attempt at structure, but lacks clear organization or is difficult to follow
0.0 No discernible structure, rambling or chaotic

Information Coverage

3.5 points

How well the introduction covers key areas for college students preparing for interviews

Key Areas to Cover:
  • Name and personal introduction
  • Academic background (degree, major, university, year of study)
  • Practical experience and projects (internships, projects, competitions, hands-on work)
  • Technical skills and competencies (programming languages, tools)
  • Career direction and goals (professional aspirations)
3.5 Comprehensively covers ALL key areas with good balance of academic and practical elements
3.0 Covers 4-5 key areas thoroughly, with good balance
2.0-2.5 Covers 3-4 key areas well, but may lack depth in practical experience or technical skills
1.0-1.5 Covers 2-3 key areas adequately, limited practical experience mentioned

Note: Higher weight given to practical experience, projects, internships, and technical skills as these demonstrate readiness.

Relevance to Professional Context

2.0 points

How well you demonstrate practical readiness and connect experience to professional work

2.0 Clearly demonstrates practical readiness and connects experience, skills, or projects to professional work context
1.5 Shows some practical preparation and makes connections to professional roles, but could be more specific
1.0 Mentions relevant skills or projects but doesn't clearly connect to professional context
0.0-0.5 Vague about practical applications or no indication of professional preparation

Focus: How well you demonstrate value you can bring to employers through practical experience.
